# CHANGE.ORG PETITION

## Title

**I'm 13. I'll be past 40 before I can afford a house. Australia needs a right to housing.**

---

## Petition Text

I'm 13 and I live in Adelaide. I'll be past 40 before I can afford a home here. Not because I won't try hard enough. Because the maths is broken.

An Adelaide house costs $940,000 today. By the time I finish uni it'll be $1.7 million. By the time I'm 27 it'll be $2.3 million. By the time I'm 40 -- if I've been saving the whole time -- it'll be over $5 million. The price moves faster than I can save. I'm not falling behind. The finish line is running away from me.

My graduate salary will be about $95,000. The deposit alone on that $1.7 million house is $340,000. If I save 20% of every pay cheque, never stop, never get sick, that takes over 20 years. By then the house costs three times more than when I started saving.

My parents' generation saved a deposit in four years. House prices grew 193% in twenty years. Wages grew 81%. Nobody fixed it. Nobody is fixing it.

135 out of 226 federal MPs own two or more properties. They spend $12.3 billion a year on tax breaks that send $7.2 billion to the richest 10%. Then they tell us to budget better.

120 countries told Australia to fix this at the UN in February. The government has until July to respond. The ACT already made housing a legal right. Victoria and Queensland have Human Rights Acts.

South Australia has nothing. No Human Rights Act. No right to housing. Nothing.

I want the SA Parliament to:

1. Pass a Human Rights Act for South Australia
2. Include the right to adequate housing
3. Give people a legal way to fight back when that right is ignored

Is a roof over your head a human right -- yes or no?

If yes, sign this. Send it to someone who needs to see these numbers. The government has until July. So do we.
