The Barriers Stack

People say "just save harder." Here's what you're actually up against. All of it. At once.

1
HECS debt

Average $28,000. Cuts your borrowing power by $50,000+. You went to uni so you could earn more. The debt from going to uni means you can borrow less. Make it make sense.

2
The rent trap

$620-$780 per week before you save a cent. Researchers surveyed 51,238 rental listings across Australia. The number affordable on Youth Allowance: zero. Not low. Zero.

3
The deposit wall

$155,000 to $321,000 depending on your city. Your parents' generation saved a deposit in four years. Yours takes twenty. Same country. Different rules.

4
Stamp duty on top

$17,000 to $55,000. On top of the deposit. A tax for buying. One to three more years of saving just for the privilege.

5
Serviceability

To actually pay the mortgage without going broke, you need a household income of $100,000 to $319,000. In Sydney that's the top 5%. The average person literally cannot afford the average house.

6
The wealth gap widens behind you

While you're stuck saving, people who already own property get 50% richer. The median homeowner is worth $1,000,000. The median renter: $60,000. Same country. 17 times apart. The game is rigged and the gap gets worse the longer you wait.
