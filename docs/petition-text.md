# CHANGE.ORG PETITION

## Title

**I'm 13. I'll be past 40 before I can afford a house. Australia needs a right to housing.**

---

## Petition Text

I'm 13 and I live in Adelaide. I'll be past 40 before I can afford a home here. Not because I won't try hard enough. Because the maths is broken.

An Adelaide house costs $940,000 today. By the time I finish uni it'll be $1.6 million. By the time I'm 40 -- if I've been saving the whole time -- it'll be $5.5 million. The price moves faster than I can save. I'm not falling behind. The finish line is running away from me.

My graduate salary will be about $92,000. The deposit alone on that $5.5 million house is $1.1 million. I will never catch up. The price grows at 6.8% a year. My wages grow at 3%. Every year the gap gets wider.

My parents' generation saved a deposit in four years. House prices grew 193% in twenty years. Wages grew 81%. Nobody fixed it. Nobody is fixing it.

135 out of 226 federal MPs own two or more properties. They spend $12.3 billion a year on tax breaks that send $7.2 billion to the richest 10%. Then they tell us to budget better.

In January 2026, over 120 countries reviewed Australia's human rights record at the UN and made 350 recommendations. In February, the UN Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights gave Australia 24 months to produce a human rights-based housing strategy. The ACT already made housing a legal right. Australia is the only liberal democracy without a national Human Rights Act.

I want the Australian Parliament to:

1. Pass a national Human Rights Act that includes the right to adequate housing
2. End negative gearing beyond one investment property
3. Scrap the capital gains tax discount for investors with multiple properties
4. Redirect that $12.3 billion into building homes people can actually afford

Is a roof over your head a human right -- yes or no?

If yes, sign this. Send it to someone who needs to see the maths. The UN gave Australia 24 months. The clock started in February 2026.
