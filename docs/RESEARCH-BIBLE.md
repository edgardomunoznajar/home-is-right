## The Headline Numbers

**By 2034, when today's Year 7 student graduates university:**

- A median Adelaide house will cost **$1.4-1.7 million**
- A median Sydney house will cost **$2.4-2.9 million**
- A median national house will cost **$1.5-1.8 million**
- Their starting salary will be approximately **$92,000**
- The price-to-income ratio will be **15-18x** nationally, **26-31x** in Sydney
- A 20% deposit on an Adelaide house will be **$277,000-$336,000**
- At current saving rates (20% of after-tax income), that deposit would take **~16-20 years** to save as a single person
- They would be **36-41 years old** before they could buy -- if they saved from day one of their career

**The killer stat for the campaign:**
> A kid starting high school today in Adelaide will be nearly 40 before they can afford to buy a home in their own city -- if house prices and wages continue at their current rates.

| 2026 | 16.4x | 12+ years |

## 2. HECS/HELP Debt

- Average HECS debt: **~$27,600** (before 20% cut applied June 2025)
- Reduces borrowing capacity by **$50,000-$95,000**
- Creates a catch-22: education is needed for higher wages, but the debt reduces housing access

## 3. The Rent Trap

- Sydney rent: **$780/week** ($40,560/year)
- Adelaide rent: **$620/week** ($32,240/year)
- After rent on median income, a young person has ~$460/week for everything else
- **More than half** of lower-income renters are in rental stress (>30% of income)
- Only **36%** of advertised rentals are affordable to a typical-income household (lowest on record)

**Anglicare Rental Affordability Snapshot 2025** (51,238 listings surveyed):
- Affordable on minimum wage: **352 listings (0.7%)**
- Affordable on JobSeeker: **3 listings**
- Affordable on Youth Allowance: **0 listings**

## 4. Mortgage Serviceability

Income needed to service a mortgage without stress (repayments <30% of gross income):

| City | Required Household Income |
|---|---|
| Sydney | $299,000-$319,000 |
| Perth | $158,613 |
| Melbourne | $140,000-$160,000 |
| Brisbane | $125,000-$140,000 |
| Adelaide | $100,000-$120,000 |

In Sydney, you need to be in the **top 5-10% of earners** to afford the median house.

## 5. Stamp Duty (on top of the deposit)

On a $1M property: $33,000-$55,000 depending on state. Extends saving timeline by 1-3 additional years.

## 6. The Compound Effect

A young Australian faces all of these simultaneously:
1. Starts with **$28,000 HECS debt** (reducing borrowing by $50K+)
2. Pays **$580-$780/week rent** (38-50% of after-tax income)
3. Needs to save **$155,000-$321,000** for deposit (12-20 years solo)
4. Plus **$17,000-$55,000** stamp duty on top
5. Then needs **$120,000-$319,000 household income** to service the mortgage
6. Meanwhile, homeowners' wealth grows 50%+ while renter wealth stagnates

## The Wealth Chasm

- Median net wealth of homeowning households: **>$1,000,000**
- Median net wealth of non-homeowning households: **$60,000**
- Ratio: **17:1**

The intergenerational housing wealth gap widened from **161% in 1998 to 234% in 2018**.

## The Bank of Mum and Dad

- **17% of first home buyers** rely on family financial aid (up from 11% in 2022)
- Average gift: **$74,040**
- **75% of parents** don't expect the money back
- Those receiving $5,000+ inheritance are **more than twice as likely** to enter homeownership
- This entrenches inequality: wealthy suburb average inheritance is ~**$3.75 million**; outer suburbs less than **$500,000**

## The Great Wealth Transfer

Over the next 20 years, **$3.5-5.4 trillion** will transfer from Boomers to younger generations -- the largest in Australian history. But it is **profoundly unequal**, concentrating homeownership further among those who already have family wealth.

# PART 5: THE POLICY LANDSCAPE

## What the Government Is Doing

### Help to Buy (launched Dec 2025)
- Government contributes up to 40% of new home / 30% of existing
- 10,000 places/year, minimum 2-5% deposit
- As of Feb 2026: 2,356 places approved, 278 completed

### Housing Australia Future Fund
- $10 billion fund targeting 40,000 social/affordable homes by 2029
- Pipeline of 55,000 homes total

### First Home Owner Grants
- Range from $10,000-$30,000 by state
- SA: $30,000 for new homes (recently increased)
- But $10,000 = just **0.6% of Sydney's median house price**
- Research shows grants create a "bring forward" effect and may push prices higher

### First Home Guarantee
- 5% deposit, no LMI, unlimited places from Oct 2025

## What's NOT Being Done

### Negative Gearing + CGT Discount
- Cost to government: **$12.3 billion/year**
- **$7.2 billion** goes to the richest 10%
- ~80% of CGT discount benefits go to top 10% of earners
- OECD (Jan 2026) explicitly recommended Australia cut both
- No concrete legislative changes enacted

### Supply
- Year 1 of Housing Accord: 174,030 homes built vs 240,000 needed (27.5% shortfall)
- Construction productivity 12% lower than 30 years ago
- No state is forecast to meet its Housing Accord target

### Politicians as Landlords
- **48 MPs and senators (21% of parliament)** own **71 rental properties**
- **48.25%** of politicians own at least one investment property (vs 15% of general population)
- **135 of 226 members** own two or more properties
- Only **16 MPs (7%)** own no property at all

## International Models That Work

### Vienna -- Social Housing
- **46% of housing** is social housing
- Nearly two-thirds of residents live in subsidised housing -- in the city centre
- Social housing drives down private rents

### Singapore -- Public Housing (HDB)
- **90% of population** in publicly governed housing
- Over 1 million high-rise units built

### Finland -- Housing First
- Long-term homelessness decreased **68%** (2008-2022)
- Homelessness reduced from 18,000 to ~3,806
- Principle: stable housing comes first, before addressing other issues

# PART 6: HOUSING AS A HUMAN RIGHT

## International Framework

- **Article 25, Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948):** Adequate housing is a fundamental right
- **Article 11.1, ICESCR (1966):** The primary UN instrument on housing rights
- **Seven elements of adequate housing** (UN): security of tenure, services, affordability, habitability, accessibility, location, cultural adequacy

## Australia's Position

- Australia is the **only liberal democracy without a national human rights act**
- The right to adequate housing is "not well protected" (AHURI)
- Only Victoria, ACT, and Queensland have state-level Human Rights Acts
- **South Australia has no Human Rights Act**

## Recent Breakthroughs

### ACT (September 2025)
- **First Australian jurisdiction** to enshrine housing as a human right in law
- Amended the Human Rights Act 2004
- Protections against arbitrary evictions, utility cut-offs, discrimination
- Commences 1 January 2027

### NSW Greens Bill (early 2026)
- Includes right to housing, scheduled for debate March 2026

### Federal Bill (June 2024)
- Senator David Pocock and Kylea Tink MP introduced a bill invoking the right to adequate housing

### UN Review (February 2026)
- Australia appeared before the **UN Human Rights Council** on 17 February 2026
- **120+ countries** called on Australia to improve housing rights
- Australia must respond within **24 months** -- creating a **July 2026 deadline** for formal response on a human rights-based housing strategy

## Public Opinion

- Housing affordability is the **#1 issue** Australians think the federal government should focus on (YouGov)
- **36% of all Australians** and **50% of young Australians (18-34)** nominate it as the top issue
- **82% of young Australians** say affordable housing is their top priority (Monash Youth Barometer)
- **85%** of young Australians report experiencing financial difficulties in the past year
- **69%** think the "Great Australian Dream" of homeownership is over
- **85%** support safe, affordable housing as a solution (YWCA)
